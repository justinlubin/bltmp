Bioinformatics Mentoring Program

Mentee Registration Form For wet lab biologists who want to learn bioinformatics skills.
Mentor Registration Form For bioinformaticians who want to volunteer as a mentor.

The Bioinformatics Mentoring Program (BMP, or “bump”) pairs wet lab biologists with bioinformaticians for long-term (1+ year) mentorship.

Research shows that we all benefit from a variety of mentors, including personal mentors, academic mentors, and so on [1, 2]. The BMP provides bioinformatics mentorship for technical, career, and personal topics.

  • Technical mentorship can include how to acquire skills in programming, statistics, or computational biology.
  • Career mentorship can include how to land a new job or decide between career paths.
  • Personal mentorship can include support for going through the struggles of learning bioinformatics, job hunting, or beyond.

The goals of this program are:

  1. To help wet lab biologists learn bioinformatics skills that aid their research.

  2. To provide a source of general career and personal mentorship for wet lab biologists.

How it works

BMP matches wet lab biology mentees with volunteer bioinformatics mentors. Ideally, this relationship should last for at least one year, with at least monthly meetings. The exact logistics of the mentoring relationship are up to the mentor and mentee.

Mentees can ask for advice of all forms. As part of this relationship, mentees can bring their wet lab data, and mentors advise them on how to analyze it, including:

  • Resources to learn programming, debugging, and computational biology.
  • Advice on how to set up a computational workflow.
  • Help with specific technical issues.

Mentees should not expect their mentor to do their work for them. Mentees bring and lead their own projects. The goal of the mentor is to teach the mentee how to successfully apply bioinformatics skills, not to do their bioinformatics analyses for them.

Important! By default, mentors are not presumed to be co-authors on any publication the mentee is working on. Mentors should only provide advice they feel comfortable providing under this pretext. Please have authorship conversations early and often.
